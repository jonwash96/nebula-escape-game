How to Play Nebula Escape

Nebula Escape is a Star Trek themed Battleships-like role-playing boardgame, using rules similar to the Salvo edition of Battleships.

Players begin by setting up their ships on the battlefield in 90 degree orientations.

Once ships are placed, if story-mode is selected, the game will navigate to a piece of the story.

The story is configured for the first player, while the second player plays the part of the enemy in battle.

Both users can choose either opposite sides, or the same side (Starfleet or Enemy).

Once the game begins, players take turns firing shots at their opponent to try & destroy their ships.

The opponent's board will be in subspace mode, allowing the player taking their turn to only see the hits & misses.

Before a user takes their turn, both boards will automatically enter subspace mode, and the player will be prompted either to enter their password, or to press a button to take their turn. This prevents the other player from cheating.

Each turn, exactly 5 shots must be placed on the opponent's board. You will not be allowed to fire with more or less. Should you need to remove a shot, simply click it again to do so.

You can place more shots if you're thinking, and remove the extra ones when you're ready to fire.

When the shot count is satisfied, the fire button becomes active. Once you press it, your board will automatically enter subspace mode, and a dialog will prompt you to continue the attack so that both players can watch as the shots are fired and evaluated.

Once all shots have been fired, a summary will appear at the top of the screen, and each shot will be recorded in the player's summary panel, along with achievements.

Achievements follow the story, and include the following:

  • First Blood: When the first ship is hit
  • First Sunken Ship: When the first ship is sunk
  • Turning Point: When 3/5 ships are sunk
  • Victory In Sight: When 4/5 ships are sunk (Called Event Horizon if the enemy gets the achievement)
  • And finally, winning the game (whether for the story-mode player or their opponent)

Each of the achievements takes you to the story mode, where a short narrative is read, and you are prompted to select from a list to make a decision.

Snippets of the story are reflected on the game dashboard after each one.

The game is won by the first player to sink all 5 of the enemy's ships.

Upon winning or losing the game, there is a final piece of the story to indulge, before returning back to the dashboard for a game summary, where you can view the final board and review each hit.

See the Controls section for how to operate the interface.

Controls

General

  • Use the 'Z' key to reboot the app in the event anything becomes out of sync. If this fails, try reloading the page.
  • Use the 'Orient' button to toggle the orientation of the boards between side-by-side & head-to-head (default).
  • Use the 'Focus' button toggle between showing 1 & 2 boards.
  • Use the pause button to access settings (see advanced).

Placing Ships

  • Use the 'Auto' button to have each ship automatically delivered to your crosshairs upon placing the previous one. (Reload the page if you need to change the positions).
  • Click on a ship from the dashboard and then hover over the board to place it.
  • Use the 'R' key on your keyboard to rotate the ships by 90 degrees.
  • Use the 'X' key to cancel an unplaced ship under the crosshairs.
  • Use the 'S' key to toggle subspace mode for the active.

Targeting Mode

  • Click a weapon from the targeting panel, then click an empty cell on the board to place it. Click a red cell to remove a placed shot. (Please do not click on white or green cells while targeting, as this can cause strange behavior.) (Some weapons only allow you to place 2 or 3 shots per turn.)
  • Use the 'Toggle Subspace' Button to show/hide your board.
  • Use the 'B' Key to toggle the targeting brackets. Hint: The little node at left is useful for identifying which cell is under the crosshairs.
  • Use the 'D' Key to toggle the targeting disk.
  • Use the 'C' key to toggle both the targeting brackets & the targeting disk simultaneously.
  • Use the 'spacebar' to pause follow-mode.

Advanced Settings (For geeks & super-users only)

  • Enable the Recovery button to interact with the app through the console. (Browser console input is unavailable, output is not).
  • Enable the dev_toggleOppSubspace button to allow the inactive board to be brought out of subspace mode on command.
  • Disable the password or change the number of clicks to 1 for switching users.

Narrative

  • Click an option to choose your answer. (Please choose only 1 answer, as sometimes choosing a second can cause strange behavior).
  • Click the 'Continue' button to advance to the next step.
  • Note: If you're curious and unsatisfied with an outcome; there are hidden easter eggs for those who seek more.
